About Me

I was born in New York City and currently live in Brooklyn. I work at the intersection of clinical data science, health informatics, health data analytics, and health benefits administration. I use data to make models and dashboards that help other people (customers, colleagues, healthcare providers, business leaders) make better business-, finance-, and health-related decisions. I also work part-time in higher education which involves teaching the skills underlying the aforementioned areas (clinical science, experimental design, qualitative and quantitative analyses, etc.) to undergraduate and graduate students. I enjoy this framework because it allows me to go back and forth between theory (in higher education) and application (in industry). What I learn each day I try to apply to my own life and share insights that may benefit others.
